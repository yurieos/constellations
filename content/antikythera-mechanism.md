In 1901, divers exploring a Roman shipwreck near the Greek island of Antikythera recovered a corroded bronze lump. It would take another century to understand what they had found: the world's first known analog computer.

The Antikythera Mechanism, built around 150-100 BCE, could predict astronomical positions, eclipses, and even the timing of the ancient Olympic Games. Its complexity of interlocking gears wouldn't be matched for over a thousand years.

## What It Tells Us

This device shatters our assumptions about ancient technology. The Greeks possessed mechanical knowledge we thought emerged only in the medieval period. What else has been lost?

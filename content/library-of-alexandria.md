The Library of Alexandria was the most famous library of the ancient world. Founded in the 3rd century BCE under Ptolemy I, it aimed to collect all the world's knowledge.

At its peak, the library held an estimated 400,000 scrolls. Scholars from across the Mediterranean came to study, debate, and advance human understanding. Euclid wrote his *Elements* here. Eratosthenes calculated the Earth's circumference with remarkable accuracy.
